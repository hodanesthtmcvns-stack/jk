**Window for intervention:** Day 0–1  
**Treatment:** Dexamethasone + urgent MRI + radiation/surgery within 24h

### **The 18 Emergency Categories**

#### **RED (Life-Threatening, Immediate Response)**

**1. Febrile Neutropenia / Neutropenic Sepsis**
- **Trigger:** Fever ≥38.3°C OR hypothermia ≤35°C OR sepsis physiology with ANC <0.5 or expected fall
- **Action:** Cultures (don't delay antibiotics), anti-pseudomonal IV antibiotic urgently, oncology + ICU review
- **Evidence:** IDSA 2011, ASCO 2018, MASCC score for risk stratification
- **Time-critical:** Door-to-antibiotic <60 min reduces mortality

**2. Septic Shock (Any Cause)**
- **Trigger:** Hypotension, rising lactate, mottling, oliguria, altered sensorium, vasopressor need
- **Action:** ABCDE, IV access, fluids/vasopressors, cultures, lactate, antibiotics per Surviving Sepsis
- **Evidence:** Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2021
- **Time-critical:** Hour-1 bundle (antibiotics, fluids, lactate)

**3. Airway Compromise / SVCS with Stridor**
- **Trigger:** Stridor, severe dyspnea, facial/neck swelling, SpO₂ fall
- **Action:** Sit up, O₂, anesthesia/ICU/ENT, consider SVCS (CT chest), airway obstruction (rigid bronch)
- **Evidence:** SVCS emergency literature, oncologic airway management
- **Time-critical:** Minutes (airway) to hours (SVCS)

**4. Metastatic Spinal Cord Compression**
- **Trigger:** New limb weakness, sensory level, severe back pain, gait change, urinary retention, bowel dysfunction
- **Action:** Immobilize if unstable, urgent whole-spine MRI, dexamethasone (if no CI), spine/rad-onc call
- **Evidence:** NICE NG234 (2023), treat as oncological emergency
- **Time-critical:** Hours; neurological outcome inversely proportional to delay

**5. Raised ICP / Seizure**
- **Trigger:** Seizure, reduced GCS, papilledema, focal deficit, severe headache/vomiting
- **Action:** Airway protection, avoid hypotension/hypoxia, urgent CT/MRI, dexamethasone if tumor edema, anticonvulsant
- **Evidence:** Neuro-oncology emergency protocols
- **Time-critical:** Minutes (seizure/herniation) to hours (edema)

**6. Tumor Lysis Syndrome**
- **Trigger:** K⁺/PO₄/uric acid rising, Ca falling, creatinine rising, arrhythmia, seizure, oliguria post-treatment
- **Action:** ECG/monitor, strict UO, repeat K/Ca/PO₄/uric acid q4–6h, hydration, treat hyperK, rasburicase/dialysis
- **Evidence:** BSH 2024, Cairo-Bishop criteria
- **Time-critical:** Hours; hyperkalemia can cause sudden cardiac death

**7. Hypercalcemia of Malignancy**
- **Trigger:** High Ca with confusion, dehydration, AKI, arrhythmia, shortened QT, severe constipation, polyuria
- **Action:** ECG, ionized/corrected Ca, RFT, hydration, calcitonin (rapid), bisphosphonate/denosumab
- **Evidence:** Endocrine Society, oncology emergency reviews
- **Time-critical:** Hours to days depending on severity

**8. Leukostasis**
- **Trigger:** Very high WBC (usually >100k) with hypoxia, respiratory distress, confusion, visual symptoms, stroke signs, priapism
- **Action:** Urgent hematology/ICU, TLS prevention, avoid non-essential PRBC pre-cytoreduction, hydroxyurea/induction chemo
- **Evidence:** Leukostasis literature (AML, ALL, CML blast crisis)
- **Time-critical:** Hours; stroke/respiratory failure risk

**9. DIC / Catastrophic Bleeding / APL**
- **Trigger:** Active bleeding, petechiae, line ooze, very low platelets, prolonged PT/aPTT, low fibrinogen
- **Action:** CBC, coags, fibrinogen, D-dimer, smear; replace components; **APL → immediate hematology + ATRA**
- **Evidence:** DIC consensus, APL treatment protocols
- **Time-critical:** Minutes to hours (hemorrhage), hours (APL coagulopathy)

**10. CAR-T / Bispecific Toxicity (CRS / ICANS)**
- **Trigger:** Recent CAR-T or T-cell engager with fever, hypotension, hypoxia, confusion, aphasia, tremor, seizure
- **Action:** ASTCT grading, infection workup, antibiotics if septic differential, tocilizumab (CRS), steroids (ICANS/severe CRS)
- **Evidence:** ASTCT consensus 2018, product-specific guidelines
- **Time-critical:** Hours; grade 1→4 can occur rapidly

**11. Severe Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor Toxicity**
- **Trigger:** ICI exposure + hypoxia, myocarditis, severe diarrhea, hepatitis, adrenal crisis, encephalitis, myasthenic syndrome, DKA
- **Action:** Hold ICI, grade toxicity, organ workup, oncology/ICU, steroids/immunosuppression per severity
- **Evidence:** ASCO 2021, NCCN ICI toxicity guidelines
- **Time-critical:** Hours to days; myocarditis/pneumonitis can deteriorate rapidly

**12. Malignant Pericardial Tamponade**
- **Trigger:** Hypotension, tachycardia, raised JVP, pulsus paradoxus, muffled heart sounds, POCUS/echo tamponade
- **Action:** Urgent echo, avoid aggressive PPV before drainage, cardiology/ICU pericardiocentesis
- **Evidence:** Malignant effusion literature
- **Time-critical:** Minutes to hours (obstructive shock)

#### **ORANGE (Urgent Same-Day Assessment)**

**13. Stable SVCS**
- **Trigger:** Facial/neck/arm swelling, venous distension, headache/dyspnea worse supine, NO stridor/coma/severe hypoxia
- **Action:** Head-up, O₂ if needed, avoid upper-limb CVC, CT chest contrast, tissue diagnosis/oncology/IR
- **Evidence:** SVCS management literature
- **Time-critical:** Same day; can decompensate if airway involved

**14. Malignant Bowel Obstruction / Typhlitis**
- **Trigger:** Vomiting, distension, obstipation, peritonism, neutropenic abdominal pain/diarrhea
- **Action:** NPO, IV fluids, NG if vomiting, CT if stable, broad-spectrum antibiotics (sepsis/neutropenia), surgery + oncology
- **Evidence:** Bowel obstruction, neutropenic enterocolitis literature
- **Time-critical:** Hours to day; perforation risk

**15. Obstructive Uropathy / Cholangitis**
- **Trigger:** AKI/anuria with hydronephrosis, obstructed infected kidney, cholangitis with biliary obstruction
- **Action:** Urgent ultrasound/POCUS, source control (urology/IR/ERCP), antibiotics if infected
- **Evidence:** Obstructive uropathy, ascending cholangitis literature
- **Time-critical:** Same day; infection with obstruction is surgical emergency

**16. Cancer-Associated VTE / PE**
- **Trigger:** Pleuritic pain, syncope, leg swelling, unexplained tachycardia/hypoxia, suspected PE without shock
- **Action:** ECG, ABG/lactate, troponin/BNP, POCUS/CTPA, anticoagulate if no CI; **massive PE → RED**
- **Evidence:** Cancer VTE guidelines (ASCO, ITAC, ISTH)
- **Time-critical:** Same day; massive PE can decompensate

**17. Extravasation / Anaphylaxis**
- **Trigger:** Infusion pain/swelling/blistering, OR urticaria/bronchospasm/hypotension during infusion
- **Action:** Stop infusion; extravasation: leave cannula, aspirate, don't flush, antidote per protocol; anaphylaxis: IM adrenaline + ABC
- **Evidence:** Chemotherapy extravasation protocols, anaphylaxis guidelines
- **Time-critical:** Minutes (anaphylaxis), hours (extravasation tissue damage)

#### **YELLOW (Expedited Review 24–48h)**

**18. Early Warning Trend / Caregiver Concern**
- **Trigger:** No immediate red/orange, but worsening trend, labs trending toward emergency, frailty, poor reserve
- **Action:** Repeat obs/labs early, document safety-net, escalate if trend persists or NEWS2/context worsens
- **Evidence:** Clinical gestalt, trend analysis literature
- **Time-critical:** 24–48 hours

### **Minimum Emergency Lab Panel**

**8-domain bundle** for unstable oncology patients:

**1. Hematology**
- CBC with differential, ANC calculation
- Peripheral smear if: leukemia, APL suspicion, hemolysis, blast concern

**2. Renal + Electrolytes**
- RFT (creatinine, BUN/urea)
- Na⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺ (ionized preferred if available), Mg²⁺, phosphate
- Repeat early if unstable or TLS risk

**3. TLS Screen** (if recent chemo, high tumor burden, or TLS risk)
- Uric acid, LDH, K⁺, PO₄, Ca²⁺, creatinine
- ECG and cardiac monitor when K⁺ or Ca²⁺ abnormal

**4. Liver**
- LFT (AST, ALT, ALP)
- Bilirubin (total and direct)
- Albumin
- Consider ammonia if unexplained encephalopathy

**5. Coagulation / Bleeding**
- PT/INR, aPTT
- Fibrinogen, D-dimer
- **Triggers:** Bleeding, sepsis, APL suspicion, DIC, pre-procedure

**6. Perfusion + Metabolic**
- ABG or VBG with lactate
- Glucose, bicarbonate
- Repeat lactate after resuscitation in shock

**7. Infection Workup** (before antibiotics when feasible)
- Blood cultures: peripheral + central line (if present)
- Urine culture (if symptoms, catheter)
- Sputum/site cultures as indicated
- **CRITICAL:** Do not delay antibiotics in suspected neutropenic sepsis

**8. Cardiorespiratory**
- ECG (always in oncology emergencies)
- CXR (infection, effusion, SVCS, pneumonitis)
- POCUS/echo/lung ultrasound as indicated
- CTPA/CT/ultrasound per stability and suspected source

## Frequently Asked Questions

### **General Questions**

**Q: Is NEWS2OncoTriage validated?**  
A: The NEWS2 component is validated (RCP 2017, n=35,585 derivation cohort). The oncology triage module is a **synthesis tool** based on published guidelines (NICE, ASCO, IDSA, BSH, ASTCT) but is **not externally validated** as a unified score. It is a cognitive assist, not a prediction model.

**Q: Can I use this for pediatric oncology patients?**  
A: No. NEWS2 is not validated in patients <16 years. Pediatric early warning scores (PEWS, CREW) exist but have different thresholds.

**Q: What about pregnant patients with cancer?**  
A: NEWS2 requires modification in pregnancy (elevated HR, lower BP, increased RR are normal). Maternal early warning systems (MEOWS) are more appropriate.

**Q: Does this work offline?**  
A: Yes. Single-file HTML, no internet required after download. All calculations in-browser.

**Q: Does it send data anywhere?**  
A: No. Zero telemetry, zero analytics, zero data transmission. Fully local.

**Q: Can I modify the code?**  
A: Yes. GNU GPL v3 license permits modification. If distributing modified versions, you must:
- Retain attribution
- Disclose source code
- Use same license (copyleft)
- Document changes clearly

### **Clinical Questions**

**Q: My patient has NEWS2 = 8 but looks well. Should I still escalate?**  
A: Yes. NEWS2 is an objective track-and-trigger system. "Looks well" is subjective and prone to bias. High scores predict deterioration even when clinical gestalt disagrees (Smith 2013). Escalate and let senior clinician make the call.

**Q: Patient has febrile neutropenia but NEWS2 = 0 (just fever 38.1°C). Tool says RED. Is that really necessary?**  
A: Yes. Neutropenic sepsis has **median time to shock 6–12 hours** from initial fever (Kuderer 2006). 30-day mortality if shock develops: 30–50%. Door-to-antibiotic <1h is IDSA strong recommendation. Do not wait for hemodynamic instability.

**Q: The tool recommends ICU call but we're a small hospital without ICU. What do I do?**  
A: Adapt to local resources. "ICU-capable response" means:
- Senior medical/ED physician
- Ability to provide high-flow O₂, fluids, vasopressors
- Monitoring capability
- Transfer pathway to higher level of care if needed

**Q: Can I use this for outpatient triage phone calls?**  
A: Limited utility. You need complete vital signs (full NEWS2 set). If only history available, use clinical gestalt + oncology context flags as screening, but full assessment requires in-person evaluation.

**Q: Patient triggered multiple RED flags. Which takes priority?**  
A: ABCDE sequence (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure). Simultaneous processes are common in oncology emergencies (e.g., neutropenic sepsis + TLS). Address life-threats first, then syndrome-specific management in parallel.

**Q: Should I delay antibiotics to get blood cultures?**  
A: **In suspected neutropenic sepsis: NO.** Obtain cultures rapidly, but if any delay in venous access or lab processing, give antibiotics first. In non-neutropenic sepsis: attempt cultures within hour-1 bundle but do not delay antibiotics >45 min (Surviving Sepsis 2021).

**Q: Patient is DNR/DNI. Should I still calculate NEWS2?**  
A: Yes. DNR/DNI addresses cardiopulmonary resuscitation, not all medical interventions. Patient may still want symptom management, antibiotics, or specific treatments. NEWS2 informs urgency of assessment; goals of care inform type of intervention.

### **Technical Questions**

**Q: Why does SpO₂ Scale 2 score higher when oxygen saturation is ABOVE target?**  
A: Type 2 respiratory failure patients (chronic CO₂ retention) have target SpO₂ 88–92%. If SpO₂ is 97% **on supplemental oxygen**, it suggests excessive FiO₂ which can worsen hypercapnia and respiratory acidosis. This is a clinical alert, not a "better oxygenation = bad" paradox.

**Q: I switched from Celsius to Fahrenheit and the score changed. Is that a bug?**  
A: No. Temperature scoring uses Celsius thresholds internally. When you switch units, the tool converts your existing value (e.g., 37.0°C → 98.6°F) and recalculates. If you then **manually adjust** the Fahrenheit slider, it will convert that new value back to Celsius for scoring. The score reflects the underlying physiological temperature, regardless of display unit.

**Q: Can I print the results?**  
A: Browser print function works (File → Print). For documentation, use "Copy TXT summary" button in Advanced Triage module and paste into EMR/clinical notes.

**Q: The gauge says score 15 but NEWS2 maximum is usually capped at 12–14. Why?**  
A: The visual gauge goes to 20 for display purposes. Clinically, any score ≥7 is high-risk. Scores >12 are rare but possible (e.g., RR 6, SpO₂ 85%, on O₂, BP 80, HR 140, Temp 34.5°C, Unresponsive = 3+3+2+3+2+3+3 = 19). The exact number matters less than the risk band (HIGH/EMERGENCY).

**Q: What browsers are supported?**  
A: All modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Works on mobile (iOS Safari, Android Chrome) and tablets. IE11 may require polyfills for const/let but generally functional.

**Q: Can I embed this in our hospital EMR?**  
A: Technically possible (iframe embed or custom integration) but requires:
- IT security review (offline execution, no external calls)
- Clinical governance approval (cognitive assist tool, not diagnostic device)
- Local validation/audit (does it improve care in your setting?)
- User training (interpretation, limitations)

## Limitations & Disclaimers

### **Clinical Limitations**

**1. Not a Diagnostic Tool**  
This tool screens for emergency categories and calculates physiological risk scores. It does not diagnose specific conditions. Definitive diagnosis requires clinical assessment, laboratory confirmation, and imaging.

**2. Not a Treatment Protocol**  
Actions listed are pathway activations (e.g., "call oncology," "urgent MRI"), not specific treatments. Actual management must follow institutional protocols and specialty guidelines.

**3. Does Not Replace Clinical Judgment**  
Algorithmic output is a cognitive assist. Clinical gestalt, trend assessment, patient-specific factors, and goals of care always take precedence.

**4. Population Limitations**  
- NEWS2 derived from general medical admissions (not oncology-specific)
- Limited validation in certain populations: elderly frail, chronic critical illness, palliative care
- No validation in pediatric (<16 years) or pregnant patients

**5. Context Modifiers Not Validated as Unified Score**  
Oncology context flags are evidence-informed but not externally validated as a scoring system. Use as clinical risk alerts, not prediction model.

**6. Advanced Triage Not Externally Validated**  
Emergency checklist synthesized from multiple guidelines but not tested as unified tool in prospective studies.

**7. Static Snapshot vs. Dynamic Trends**  
Tool provides point-in-time assessment. Serial measurements and trend analysis are clinically superior for deterioration detection.

**8. Does Not Account for Baseline Variability**  
Some patients have chronic vital sign abnormalities (e.g., baseline tachycardia, hypotension from autonomic neuropathy). Tool does not adjust for individual baselines.

---

### **Technical Limitations**

**1. Offline Only — No Cloud Integration**  
Cannot sync data across devices, share scores remotely, or integrate with EMR automatically. Manual transcription required for documentation.

**2. No Long-Term Data Storage**  
Calculations are in-memory only. Closing browser tab loses data. No patient database or longitudinal tracking.

**3. No Decision Support AI**  
Rule-based logic only. No machine learning, no predictive modeling beyond standard NEWS2 risk bands.

**4. Limited Accessibility Features**  
Screen reader optimization incomplete. May not meet full WCAG 2.1 AAA standards for visually impaired users.

**5. Manual Data Entry**  
Requires manual vital sign entry. No automated import from monitors or EMR.

## Privacy & Data

### **What Data is Collected?**

**None.**

This tool:
- ❌ Does not transmit data to servers
- ❌ Does not store patient information
- ❌ Does not use cookies (except localStorage for UI preferences)
- ❌ Does not track usage analytics
- ❌ Does not require login or user accounts

### **What Happens to Entered Data?**

- Vital signs and selections are held in **browser memory only** (JavaScript variables)
- Closing the browser tab **permanently deletes** all entered data
- No data persists between sessions (unless user manually saves TXT export)

### **LocalStorage Use**

The tool stores **one non-clinical preference** in browser localStorage:
- **Key:** `news2oncotriage_screen_mode`
- **Value:** `'mobile'` or `'pc'`
- **Purpose:** Remember user's layout preference (mobile vs. desktop view)
- **Can be cleared:** Browser settings → Clear browsing data → Cookies and site data

### **TXT Export**

When user clicks "Copy TXT summary":
- Text is copied to clipboard (browser Clipboard API or fallback `document.execCommand`)
- User controls where pasted (EMR, notes, email, etc.)
- No copy is retained by the tool

**If case number entered:** User-entered case number appears in export. User is responsible for HIPAA/GDPR compliance when sharing.

- **HIPAA (USA):** Tool itself is compliant (no data storage/transmission). Clinical use must follow institutional HIPAA policies.
- **GDPR (EU):** No personal data processing occurs. If user exports data containing patient identifiers, standard GDPR protections apply.

## Final Notes

### **Philosophy of This Tool**

**1. Cognitive Assist, Not Autopilot**  
Algorithms support thinking; they do not replace it. This tool structures assessment, flags risks, and prompts actions — but the clinician remains the decision-maker.

**2. Evidence-Informed, Not Evidence-Dictated**  
Where high-quality evidence exists (NEWS2, IDSA FN guidelines), we follow it precisely. Where evidence is weak or absent (optimal oncology context weighting), we make reasonable evidence-informed choices and clearly state limitations.

**3. Open and Transparent**  
All scoring logic is visible in the code. No black boxes. If you disagree with a threshold or action, you can see why it's there, check the reference, and modify if needed (GPL v3 allows this).

**4. Safety First**  
When uncertain, the tool errs toward escalation. Better to over-triage and let senior clinicians de-escalate than to under-triage and miss deterioration.

**5. Evolving with Evidence**  
As guidelines update (e.g., BSH TLS 2024, ASCO ICI 2021 → future 2025+), this tool should be updated. Version control and open-source model support continuous improvement.

**For Clinicians:**

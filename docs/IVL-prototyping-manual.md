# An Open-Source (GPL-3.0) Technical Manual for Building an Improvised Video Laryngoscope (IVL) for Manikin-Based Airway Training

**Document Version:** 6 Feb 2026  
**Author:** Prof. J. Kirtania  
**License:** GNU General Public License v3.0 (GPL-3.0)

---

This is a verified technical manual for building improvised video laryngoscope (IVL) devices for widespread low-cost adoption and training of videolaryngoscopy for anesthesia postgraduate trainee doctors and ICU resident doctors on intubation manikins (e.g., Ambu Airway Man).

**This is not a regulator-approved medical device for use on patients.**

## B. Step-by-Step Prototyping Procedure (Using Exact Tools)

### 0) Bench Setup
- Work on a stable table with good lighting.
- Keep the laryngoscope blade fixed (vise/clamp or held firmly) during test-fitting.
- Wear protective goggles for eye protection (rotary tool dust).

---

### 1) Prepare the Blade Slot (Important for Tie Durability)

**Goal:** Remove burrs that can cut nylon ties.

- Use needle files (flat / half-round) to lightly deburr the elongated slot edges (inside and outside).
- Finish with light smoothing (sandpaper optional; file alone is acceptable).
- **Confirm:** No sharp edge catches a cotton swab.

---

### 2) Prepare the ETT Housing Segment

**Goal:** Make a rigid “camera cartridge” that will not rotate.

1. Cut a short ETT segment (typical workable length: 30–40 mm).

2. **Longitudinal D-flat (Anti-rotation)**
   - Mark a straight longitudinal line where the flat will be created.
   - Using a UniQual sanding drum at **low RPM**, remove material to create a flat:
     - Flat depth: ~0.6–0.8 mm  
     - Flat width: ~4–6 mm
   - Finalize with a flat needle file until the segment sits stably against one side of the blade slot (D-profile effect).

**Tool technique (plastic):**
- Low RPM, very light pressure, short contact time to avoid melting.
- If plastic begins to smear, stop and allow cooling.

---

### 3) Cut Circumferential Grooves for Cable Ties

**Goal:** Seat the 2.5 mm tie so it cannot “walk” along the tube.

- Mark **minimum 2 grooves** (3 ideal):
  - Groove 1: ~5–7 mm from distal end
  - Groove 2: ~20 mm proximal to groove 1
  - Groove 3 (optional): ~15–18 mm proximal to groove 2
- Use a diamond burr that produces ~2.5–3.0 mm groove width.

**Groove dimensions:**
- Width: 2.5–3.0 mm  
- Depth: ~0.5 mm

- Smooth each groove using a round or half-round needle file to remove sharp edges.

**Check:** A 2.5 mm cable tie should sit inside the groove, not perched on its edges.

---

### 4) Assemble Camera into the ETT Cartridge

- Insert the snake camera into the ETT segment.
- Set camera protrusion:
  - Lens flush with distal end **or**
  - 1–2 mm beyond distal end
- Lock preliminary orientation:
  - Use OTG app live view to ensure correct “up” orientation.
  - Temporarily tape the cable to prevent twist during tying.

---

### 5) Position on Blade and Tie Down

- Place the ETT + camera cartridge along the elongated slot:
  - Camera near distal curvature
  - Lens positioned ~15–25 mm proximal to blade tip
  - Blade tip should be **just visible** at the top of the field of view.
- Angle the camera lens slightly **upwards** (toward blade tip).
- Keep the D-flat on the **inner curvature side** so the cartridge nests into the Macintosh flange, preserving oral working space.
- Mark the **12 o’clock** orientation on the ETT housing.

**Apply cable ties (2–3):**
- Route ties to compress the cartridge against the flange without bulky projections.
- Align locking heads within the slot shadow or along the flange curvature (least snag).
- Tighten gradually:
  - Lightly tighten tie #1 and tie #2.
  - Re-check live orientation.
  - Fully cinch once orientation is correct.

**Field of View (FOV) Check – Rule of Thirds:**
- Top 1/3: Blade tip visible
- Bottom 2/3: Clear glottic view (manikin)

**Key point:**  
The D-flat must bear against the slot wall. Cable ties provide compression, not primary anti-rotation.

---

### 6) Trim and Round Tie Ends

- Cut tie tails as close as possible using flush cutters.
- Do **not** use diagonal cutters or standard wire cutters.
- Round cut ends using a soldering iron (“heat-kiss” technique):
  - Bring side of tip close; do not press.
  - Stop once nylon domes.
  - Avoid heating the ratchet head.

**Pass/fail test:**  
A gloved finger should not detect any sharp or hook-like edges.

---

### 7) Cable Strain Relief

- Route camera cable along the handle.
- Add one additional cable tie (or tape) as strain relief so cable pull does not torque the camera.

---

### 8) Phone + IV Pole “Video Tower”

- Mount phone on clamp attached to IV pole at chest-to-eye level.
- Manage cable slack; avoid downward pull on blade end.
- Lock phone orientation and disable auto-rotate.

---

### 9) Mechanical Torture Test (Before Manikin Use)

- Firmly pull, twist, and lever the camera cartridge by hand.
- If movement occurs:
  - Increase tie tension
  - Reconfirm D-flat contact
  - Add a third tie if needed
- Re-check view orientation afterward.

---

### 10) Pro Tip

- To prevent lens fogging, apply a tiny drop of baby shampoo or commercial anti-fog solution and buff clear before use.

---

## QC Check: “3-Point Shake Test”

Before handing the device to a resident, perform all three:

1. **Sight Test**  
   Confirm the 12 o’clock mark aligns with “Up” on the screen.

2. **Torque Test**  
   Attempt to rotate the camera housing. Any movement ≥1 mm indicates inadequate D-flat depth or tie tension.

3. **Snag Test**  
   Run a silk suture or thin cloth across all tie points. Any catching indicates incomplete rounding.

## Cleaning and Disinfection of Blade with Camera Assembly

### 1) Cleaning (Decontamination)
- Rinse immediately under cool running water.
- Clean with enzymatic cleaner or detergent using a soft brush.
- Rinse with lukewarm water.
- Dry with a lint-free cloth.

### 2) Disinfection (After Cleaning and Drying)
- **ETO sterilization** is preferred.

**High-Level Disinfection (HLD) options:**
- 2% glutaraldehyde or ortho-phthalaldehyde (OPA) for 20 minutes  
- Rely+On™ PeraSafe (16.2 g/L) for 10 minutes  
- Virkon® S 2% solution for 10 minutes

Metabolic Life-History Simulator

Your heart has a budget of roughly 2.5 billion beats. How you spend it -- and how you strengthen it -- determines longevity. This model distinguishes three physiologically distinct activity tiers: voluntary exercise, activities of daily living (ADL), and acute survival stress. Each has a different biological cost and adaptive value.

Model notes -- The 2.5 × 10⁹ beat budget is an empirical cross-species allometric constant. ADL bursts contribute a partial adaptive benefit (~40% of equivalent exercise dose) because they lack warm-up, cool-down, and structured recovery. Survival stress events cost cardiac beats directly (high HR spike) but also impose a cortisol-mediated allostatic penalty: each event under chronic stress load shortens recovery and degrades vagal tone over time. This is modelled as a multiplicative penalty on the beat budget. Afterload penalty applies above 80 mmHg MAP. Not a clinical tool. Educational tool for learning physiology.
